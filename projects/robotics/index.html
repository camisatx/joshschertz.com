5 DoF Robot Arm

Arm Construction

Constructing the arm was a multi-step process. The first step covered the basic arm build. To increase the arm's stability, I added a rotatable platform in the second step. The third step covered integrating all of the servos and electronics. I briefly covered basic code in step five.

CAD Model

Even though I have a working physical arm, I needed a CAD model of the arm so that I could perform Gazebo simulations. Simulations are useful for building the kinematic algorithms to control and move the arm.

I used Fusion 360 to design the arm from scratch, except for the servos and the Arduino Mega. You can explore the model here:

Each arm joint is able to rotate, including each gripper link. I estimate that the model has an measurement error tolerance of 0 - 1 mm per component (I had to use a ruler to measure each item since I didn't have a caliper).

Pneumatic Grippers

In order to provide a better mechanism for picking up objects, I am experimenting with pneumatic grippers, including the jamming and soft grippers. These grippers use soft rubbers to interface with objects, meaning delicate objects can be manipulated without damage. The grippers are also easier to orient when picking up an object.
